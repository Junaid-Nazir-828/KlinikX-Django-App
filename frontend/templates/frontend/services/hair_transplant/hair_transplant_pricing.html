How Many Grafts Do You Need?

The number of grafts needed for a hair transplant depends on the extent of your hair loss and the desired level of coverage. One common way to classify male pattern baldness is through the Norwood Scale, which has several classes representing different stages of hair loss.

Norwood Classes and Graft Estimation

Each Norwood class represents a different stage of hair loss. The number of grafts needed to restore your natural look will vary based on your specific Norwood class. Here's an estimation of grafts required for each class:

  • Norwood Class I: Approximately 0-400 grafts.
  • Norwood Class II: Approximately 400-800 grafts.
  • Norwood Class IIa: Approximately 800-1200 grafts.
  • Norwood Class IIIa: Approximately 1200-1500 grafts.
  • Norwood Class III: Approximately 1500-2000 grafts.
  • Norwood Class IIIvertex: Approximately 2000-2500 grafts.
  • Norwood Class IV: Approximately 2500-3000 grafts.
  • Norwood Class IVa: Approximately 3000-3400 grafts.
  • Norwood Class V: Approximately 3400-4000 grafts.
  • Norwood Class Va: Approximately 4000-4500 grafts.
  • Norwood Class VI: Approximately 4500-5000 grafts.
  • Norwood Class VII: Approximately 5000-6000+ grafts.

It's important to note that these numbers are approximate and may vary depending on individual factors and expectations. To get an accurate assessment of the number of grafts you need, we recommend scheduling a consultation with our experienced hair transplant specialists at KlinikX.
