Direct Hair Implantation (DHI)

DHI is a cutting-edge hair transplant technique that provides significant benefits for patients seeking natural hair restoration. The procedure involves extracting hair follicles one by one using a Choi pen, ensuring precise and accurate implantation into the recipient area. DHI offers minimal scarring, faster recovery, and natural-looking results. Patients undergo the procedure with local anesthesia, ensuring a comfortable and pain-free experience.

DHI Hair Transplant Technique

  • Variation of FUE where hair follicles are collected using a micro-motor and transplanted with a "Choi pen".
  • No scarring, bleeding, or traces of transplantation after the operation.
  • Does not require leaving grafts exposed, reducing the risk of hair root damage.
  • No incisions or stitches needed after the procedure.
  • Provides more natural-looking results as the surgeon has full control over the depth, direction, and angle of the implantation.

DHI Process

The DHI process consists of three main stages: consultation, extraction, and implantation. During the consultation, the surgeon will assess your hair loss condition and develop a personalized treatment plan. The extraction phase involves removing individual hair follicles from the donor area. These extracted follicles are then implanted in the recipient area using a Choi pen.

Preparation and Aftercare Program

Before the procedure, patients are advised to avoid blood thinners, herbal teas, alcohol, and smoking to optimize the success of the transplant. After the procedure, it is essential to get plenty of rest, avoid strenuous activities, and follow specific guidelines for washing and caring for the transplant area.

At KlinikX, we prioritize the well-being of our patients even after the procedure. Our comprehensive aftercare program includes continuous monitoring, follow-up appointments, and the provision of specialized aftercare products to enhance the results of the hair transplant.

Advantages of DHI

  • No need for incisions: Unlike FUT, DHI does not require the removal of a strip of scalp and does not leave a linear scar. This makes it a more attractive option for individuals who prefer to keep their hair short.
  • Higher graft survival rate: The direct implantation process minimizes the time hair follicles are kept outside the body, which can improve the survival rate of transplanted grafts.
  • Quicker recovery: The minimally invasive nature of DHI typically leads to a faster recovery period compared to more invasive hair transplant procedures.
  • Lower risk of infection or complications as the procedure does not involve any major surgical cuts or stitches.

Potential Risks and Costs of DHI

Like all surgical procedures, DHI comes with some potential risks including infection, minor bleeding, and temporary numbness in the treated areas. However, these risks can be minimized by following post-op care instructions carefully. The cost of DHI varies based on several factors including the severity of hair loss, the number of grafts required.

DHI vs Other Methods

DHI offers several advantages over traditional methods like FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) and FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction). Unlike FUT, DHI does not involve removing a strip of scalp, thereby avoiding a linear scar. Compared to FUE, DHI offers greater precision as each hair follicle is implanted directly without the need for creating recipient holes beforehand. This means less trauma to the scalp, faster recovery, and more natural-looking results.

Additionally, DHI can be performed under local anesthesia, reducing the risks associated with general anesthesia. However, it is important to note that the success of a hair transplant, regardless of the method used, also depends on the expertise of the surgeon and the patient's adherence to aftercare instructions.

Frequently Asked Questions about DHI

Those who have healthy hair growth at the back and sides of the head to serve as donor areas can be good candidates for DHI. It can also be an ideal solution for those who wish to wear their hair short as it does not leave a linear scar.

The duration of the DHI procedure depends on the number of grafts required. Typically, it can take anywhere between 6 to 8 hours.

Initial results may be seen as early as three months after the procedure. However, full growth can take between 9 to 12 months.
