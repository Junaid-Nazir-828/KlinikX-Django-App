SMILE - Minimally Invasive Vision Correction

SMILE (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) is a minimally invasive and flapless laser eye surgery used to treat myopia (nearsightedness) and astigmatism. The procedure involves creating a small, precise lenticule inside the cornea, which is then removed to reshape the cornea and correct the refractive error.

SMILE Procedure

  • Creation of a small lenticule inside the cornea using a femtosecond laser.
  • Removal of the lenticule through a small incision, reshaping the cornea.
  • Flapless and minimally invasive procedure.
  • Quick and comfortable recovery.
  • Effective vision correction.

Advantages of SMILE

  • Minimally invasive with a small incision.
  • Less disruption to corneal nerves, reducing dry eye symptoms.
  • Rapid visual recovery.
  • Less risk of corneal ectasia.

The SMILE Procedure

TIn the SMILE procedure, a femtosecond laser is used to create a small disc-shaped piece of tissue (lenticule) within the cornea. This lenticule is then removed through a small incision in the surface of the cornea. By removing this lenticule, the cornea is reshaped, correcting the way it refracts light and thus improving vision.

Candidate Suitability for SMILE

Candidates for SMILE surgery should be at least 22 years old with a stable eyeglass prescription for at least one year. They should have moderate to high myopia, with or without astigmatism. SMILE may not be suitable for individuals with certain eye or health conditions that could impact healing, such as severe dry eye, glaucoma, or autoimmune diseases. A thorough eye examination is required to evaluate suitability for the procedure.

Potential Risks and Side Effects of SMILE

Like all surgical procedures, SMILE involves some risks and potential side effects. These may include dry eyes, glare, light sensitivity, and seeing halos around lights. In rare cases, the need for a second surgery (enhancement procedure) might arise if the desired vision correction is not achieved. However, serious complications are relatively rare and most side effects are typically temporary and diminish over time.

Post-Operative Care and Recovery

Post-operative care after SMILE is crucial for successful healing and optimal vision correction. It involves using prescribed eye drops, wearing protective eyewear to prevent rubbing or pressure, and attending follow-up appointments to monitor healing. The majority of patients experience a quick recovery, with noticeable vision improvement within a few days.

Comparing SMILE with Other Corrective Eye Surgeries

Although SMILE is a relatively new entrant in the field of refractive surgery, it has been favorably compared to established procedures such as LASIK and PRK. Its minimally invasive approach reduces certain risks associated with LASIK, like flap-related complications, and tends to result in less postoperative dryness than both LASIK and PRK. However, the best procedure for any given individual depends on a variety of factors, including their specific vision problems, lifestyle, and overall eye health.

Future Developments in SMILE Technology

Advances in refractive surgery continue to improve outcomes for patients, and SMILE is at the forefront of these developments. The potential future of SMILE includes its application for the correction of higher degrees of astigmatism and hyperopia (farsightedness). Ongoing research and clinical trials are expected to further expand the use and capabilities of the SMILE procedure.
